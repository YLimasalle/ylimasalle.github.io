Howdy!

I'm just a guy who grew up playing too many video games that turned into a fascination of computer science. I then followed that fascination all the way to a bachelor's degree. Currently my focus with coding revolves around empowering individuals to achieve their goals through apps and experiences.

A music discorvery app, tailored for small local artists

Overview

Oxchord is a social music platform for artists looking to build a local community of fans and other music makers. Post your music from whatever platform you upload on, and hear music from the platforms you don't subscribe to.

Our goal is to make music discovery and artist appreciation easier for everyone. No matter why you love music, we want to make a place where fans can find it, and artists can finally get heard.

Tech Stack

  • Ionic React
  • ASP.NET Core
  • PostgreSQL
  • AWS Cognito + S3

Overview

Inspired by the best way to warm intro friends and born out of the social isolation of COVID, we made Trndapp. Trndapp is an app that lets your close-knit group of friends go out and meet other groups of friends. No anxious one-on-one encounters, just a fun night out with people you know and people you can get to know.

A journaling and self-reflection chrome extension

Overview

Heavily influenced by the idea of mindfulness and self-reflection (and especially this series of interviews by Vanity Fair), Metanoia is a journaling extension that prompts the user daily with quick reflective sentiments. It provides a non-committal way to jot down your thoughts and feelings without the pressure of maintaining a traditional journal.

Won 1st place at the CU Hackathon X in the UI/UX track.

Planning chair for the Society of Asian Scientists and Engineers' largest regional conference

Overview

After planning and executing the Mountain region's 2019 conference, was asked to help with the 2020 NorthEast Regional Conference. Our main goal was to provide members of the organization a deeply practical and useful experience that would help them further their career. We also made sure to make sure the event was worthwhile to our sponsors by providing them with access to a talent pool of over 500+ students from across the region. Additionally we made sure to extend the boundaries set by previous conferences by introducing seminars, workshops, and expereinces that were new to SASE conferences as a whole (suit measurements, one-on-one company representative meetings, and soft-skill focused workshops).
